Kiosk Step 2 — Alternatives

error What's wrong with the current flow

Fundamental: Two screens for one decision

After selecting a service, the customer hits a phone numpad screen — but hasn't received their ticket yet. They're being asked to make a notification choice before knowing their position or wait time. The actual ticket only appears on a second screen, which the customer reaches only after interacting with (or skipping) the numpad.

Problem breakdown

  • Flow The phone screen blocks ticket creation. The customer can't see their position until after they deal with the SMS prompt.
  • Hierarchy An optional feature (SMS) is presented as the primary action. The numpad dominates 70% of screen real estate.
  • Context When deciding about SMS, the customer doesn't know their wait time. "Do I need an SMS?" depends on whether the wait is 5 min or 45 min.
  • Clarity Three notification methods exist (TV display, QR code, SMS) but only SMS is presented. QR appears later on the ticket screen as an afterthought.
  • CTA Screen 1 has "Prendre un ticket" + "Continuer sans telephone" — two ways to do the same thing. Screen 2 has "Nouveau ticket" which is for the next customer, not this one.
  • Print There's no option to print a physical ticket. In a bank, many customers (especially older ones) expect a printed slip.

lightbulb The fix: Merge into one screen

After selecting a service, the API creates the ticket immediately. The customer sees one screen that:

  • Confirms they've joined the queue (green checkmark)
  • Shows their ticket number, service, position, and estimated wait
  • Presents all 3 notification options clearly and equally:
    1. Ecran TV — follow your number on the screen in the waiting area (default, no action needed)
    2. QR Code — scan with your phone to track your position live
    3. SMS — enter your number to be notified when it's your turn

Below are 3 alternative layouts for this single screen, all following SG design guidelines.

A: Split Panel — Confirmation left, actions right

The left panel is a calm, spacious ticket confirmation. The right panel is a focused "action zone" with 3 clear options. The customer sees everything at once without scrolling. Tapping an option could expand it inline (e.g., SMS shows a numpad).

PRO Clear separation of "info" vs "actions". Spacious, easy to scan.
CON Right panel may feel narrow on smaller kiosk screens.

B: Tab Switcher — Compact header, switchable content

Ticket info is condensed into a compact horizontal strip at the top. Below, 3 tabs let the customer explore each notification method. The default tab is "Ecran TV" (no action required). The customer can switch to QR or SMS if they want more.

PRO Maximum space for each option. SMS numpad fits naturally. Progressive disclosure.
CON Tabs require the user to discover options. Not all 3 visible at once.

C: All-in-One Card — Everything in one centered card

A single card with the ticket confirmation on top and 3 equal-weight notification options below. Each option is a large, tappable card. Tapping one navigates to its detail (e.g., full QR code, SMS numpad). This is the simplest and most "kiosk-native" approach.

PRO All 3 options visible at equal weight. Very simple. Works on any screen size. Most kiosk-like.
CON Less space for detail per option. SMS requires a sub-step.
