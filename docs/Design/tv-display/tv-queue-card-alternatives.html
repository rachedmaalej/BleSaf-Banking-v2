Queue Card Alternatives - Customer-Focused Design

Optimizing the "Prochains en file" cards for waiting customers who need to quickly find their position and estimated wait time

psychology Customer Psychology Insight

A customer scanning the TV display has two primary questions: "Where am I in the queue?" and "How long until my turn?" The ticket number is secondary — they already know it from their ticket. The design should prioritize position number and estimated wait time as the hero elements, making them instantly scannable from across the room.

1
Position-First Hero
Recommended

The position number takes center stage in a bold colored circle, immediately answering "Where am I?". The wait time is displayed as a prominent pill badge just below. The ticket number becomes tertiary information at the bottom.

Position Visibility Large circled number is scannable from 10+ meters
Wait Time Prominence Colored badge draws attention after position
Visual Hierarchy Clear 1-2-3 reading order: position → time → ticket
Brand Alignment Service color in circle reinforces category

Dual-Hero Split Layout
Bold

The card is split into two equal sections: position on the left (with service color background) and wait time on the right. Both pieces of critical information are given equal visual weight. The ticket number and service icon are relegated to a compact footer.

Equal Emphasis Position and time are equally prominent
Scanning Pattern Left-to-right natural reading flow
Dense Information All key data visible without scrolling eye
Clear Separation Visual division prevents confusion

Countdown Focus with Progress
Modern

The wait time becomes the dominant hero element with large, light-weight typography. A progress bar shows visual advancement toward service. The position is shown as a compact badge in the top-left, while the ticket number is displayed prominently in the header alongside it.

Time-Centric Wait time is the largest element on the card
Progress Visualization Bar shows advancement, reduces anxiety
Compact Position Position badge is small but colored for visibility
Modern Feel Light typography gives premium, calm aesthetic

lightbulb Recommendation

Alternative 1 (Position-First Hero) is recommended for BléSaf's banking context. The large colored position circle is instantly scannable from across the lobby, allowing customers to quickly locate their position without squinting. The wait time pill badge provides the second most important information prominently. This design follows the natural customer question flow: "Where am I?" → "How long?" → "Is that my ticket?"
