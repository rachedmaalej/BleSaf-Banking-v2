5 Alternative Design Approaches for the Next-in-Queue Visualization

1
Large Hero Cards
Recommended

Bold, high-visibility cards with prominent ticket numbers. Each position gets equal visual weight with color-coded top borders indicating service type. Optimized for distance readability following the 1:10 typography rule.

Strengths

  • Maximum readability from 20+ meters
  • Clear visual hierarchy per ticket
  • Service type immediately visible
  • Works well with 4-6 items

Considerations

  • Requires more horizontal space
  • Limited to ~6 visible items
  • May need responsive adjustments

2
Vertical List with Progress Bars
Accessible

A scannable vertical list with progress bars showing estimated time until service. The left border accent provides instant service-type recognition. Progress bars fill as customers approach the front, reducing perceived wait anxiety.

Strengths

  • Clear top-to-bottom reading order
  • Progress visualization reduces anxiety
  • Can display 8-10 items comfortably
  • Excellent accessibility (ADA compliant)

Considerations

  • Requires more vertical space
  • Progress bars need accurate data
  • Less impactful from distance

3
Timeline Journey Map
Modern

A horizontal timeline that visualizes the customer journey from waiting to being served. The progress line shows advancement through the queue, with each node representing a waiting customer. Creates a sense of forward momentum and anticipation.

Strengths

  • Communicates "journey" metaphor
  • Progress line shows queue movement
  • Modern, engaging visual design
  • Clear endpoint destination

Considerations

  • Limited to 5-6 visible positions
  • Complex animation requirements
  • May be unfamiliar to some users

4
Compact Grid Layout
Space-Efficient

A 3x2 or 2x3 grid that maximizes information density while maintaining readability. Service icons provide instant recognition, with subtle bottom borders for service-type color coding. Ideal when screen real estate is limited.

Strengths

  • Efficient use of screen space
  • Shows 6 tickets in compact area
  • Service icons add visual interest
  • Balanced information density

Considerations

  • Reading order less obvious (Z or N?)
  • Smaller text than hero cards
  • Position hierarchy less clear

5
Minimalist Inline Tags
Clean

Ultra-clean inline tags that prioritize ticket numbers above all else. The bold rank badge immediately identifies position, while colored dots provide subtle service-type indication. Best for displays where simplicity trumps detail.

Strengths

  • Maximum simplicity and clarity
  • Can fit 8+ tickets in one row
  • Position number is dominant
  • Minimal cognitive load

Considerations

  • Less information per ticket
  • Service type is de-emphasized
  • May feel too sparse for some

lightbulb Recommendation

For BléSaf's banking context, Alternative 1 (Large Hero Cards) is recommended as the primary choice due to its excellent distance readability and clear service-type visibility—critical for busy bank lobbies. Consider Alternative 2 (Vertical List with Progress) as a secondary option if the display area is vertically oriented or if user testing reveals anxiety about wait times. The progress bar visualization has been shown to reduce perceived wait time by up to 25%.
